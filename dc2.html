Design Challenge 2

Run. Hide. Fight.

Ideation Execution Final Book

Ideation

I wanted to do something... meta. Or at least that is the conclusion I came to.


My first step in approaching this project was to make a mind map (below). As you can see, I branched out in many different categories, including photography itself. Here you can see my idea for taking photos of other photographers.

Over time, my photo-in-photo idea expanded. I thought it would be cool to show the photo being taken by the subject inside my photo, so when I began shooting, I asked them to take photos too.

I asked my subjects to tell me their favorite places to be in my hometown of Kittery, Maine. I then drove them to said place and we did our shoots there. As I saw them taking pictures, so focused on their surroundings, it hit me. What if instead of just focusing on the photos, I also focused on the lifestyle surrounding photography?

This is when the idea of Our Digital Age came to be. Society is so focused now of days on social media, and how many likes we can get. But we don't seem to focus on the actual things we are taking pictures of... or at least as much as we should.

The black and white imagery surrounding a colorful photo represents that we are so focused on the image, we can't see the big picture surrounding us. 

I shoot on a Sony a6500, I mainly use it for my film endeavors, but this was an exception. I was pleased with the images it produced. My subjects shot on phones with the exception of John, he had his own DSLR camera.

I edited the photos completely inside of Adobe Photoshop CC 2019. I didn't feel like destroying my hard drives, so I stuck to .jpg's through the process. You might be wondering how I creating the 3D effect. I used the Vanishing Point tool, and a lot of time and finess to get everything right. Google it if you're interested.

The book was made in Adobe Lightroom CC 2019.

Documentary Project

Life in a Pandemic

Artist Statement Photos

Artist Statement

For this project, I chose to observe my girlfriend Mikayleigh, and my roommate Mark, both of whom are living with me during the COVID-19 quarantine. I believe that there are two sides to every coin, and I wanted to capture that. So first, I shot each of them candid, stuck inside doing work. This represents the dark, stressful side of the outbreak. Next, I asked them to de-stress by doing something that would give them relief. They both chose to go outside, Mikayleigh going on a run, and Mark on a Boosted Board ride. This represents the silver lining: we get lots of opportunities to do these types of activities throughout the day, as long as we are being safe. This is a difficult time, and we need to focus on staying happy and sane.

Photos

All photos were shot with a Sony a6500 mirrorless camera, and edited in Photoshop 2020. The files here are formatted for the web, you can access the originals on Google Drive.
All photos were shot handheld, from 12pm-2pm, under partly cloudy skies.
